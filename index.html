Agricultural Data Analysis

Synopsis

This project explores a crop yield dataset provided on Kaggle by Samuel Oti Attakorah. This dataset provides a comprehensive view aimed at predicting crop yield (in tonnes per hectare) based on several factors. Each entry in the dataset corresponds to a unique crop with its unique profile, such as the region where it was grown, the soil type, the amount of rainfall, etc, offering a detailed breakdown of how these factors could affect yield. The dataset originally consisted of 1 million rows, but was scaled down to 500 due to technical limitations such as lags and crashes.

The transformed dataset, raw SPSS Output file, and a Results and Discussions Word document written in APA style, which goes in depth and is more aimed at a technical audience, are presented in my GitHub

This website contains the visualisations of the results and a quick and easily digestible summary for a non-technical audience.

Region

Summary

A total of 500 observations were analysed. The regional distribution of the data showed that the largest proportion of samples came from the North (32%), followed by the West (24.4%), South (23%), and East (20.6%).

Soil Type

Soil Type

Summary

Silt was the most commonly used soil type (18%), closely followed by clay (17.8%), loam (17.6%), sandy (16.6%), peaty (15.8%), and chalky (14.2%).

Crop Grown

Crop Grown

Summary

Crop distribution was relatively even, with cotton being the most frequently cultivated (17.8%), and soybeans the least (15%). Other major crops included rice (17.6%), barley (16.6%), wheat (17.4%), and maize (15.6%).

Weather Condition

Summary Statistics

Summary Statistics

Summary

The average rainfall across all observations was 542.70 mm. Mean temperature was 27.68°C, and the average time to harvest was 105.32 days. The average yield was 4.65 tons per hectare.

Total Yield per Crop(tonnes)

Total Yield per Crop(tonnes)

Summary

Rice had the highest yield at 422.78 tonnes. Cotton followed second with a yield of 404.67. Wheat trailed behind at just 403.32. Maize was 380.71, Barley had a yield of 365.20, and finally, Soybean had the lowest at 348.59. Since Rice and maize showed high mean yields, consider incentivizing these crops in suitable areas, especially where rainfall and temperature are favorable.

Yield per hectare by Fertiliser Used?

Independent Samples T-Test (Yield by Fertiliser Used?)

Summary

The box and whisker plot above compares average yield per hectare based on whether a fertiliser was applied. It helps assess whether fertiliser application leads to higher yield per hectare.

From this visualization, we can observe that there was a significant difference in average yield obtained for whether fertiliser was used or not. When fertiliser was used, it led to a higher yield compared to when it was not applied. So, promotion of the useage of fertilisers should be encouraged as it significantly improve yield. Training and subsidies could boost adoption in underutilizing areas.
